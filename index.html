When to Seek Immediate Help

  • Any mention of suicide or self-harm
  • Psychotic symptoms (hallucinations, delusions)
  • Unable to care for themselves
  • Danger to others
  • Severe substance abuse

Understanding Mental Health Diagnoses

Common conditions in young women

Major Depressive Disorder

Key Symptoms

  • Persistent sadness or empty mood
  • Loss of interest in activities
  • Changes in appetite/weight
  • Sleep disturbances
  • Fatigue or loss of energy
  • Feelings of worthlessness or guilt
  • Difficulty concentrating
  • Thoughts of death or suicide

Discipleship Considerations

  • Depression is a medical condition, not a spiritual failing
  • Encourage professional treatment alongside spiritual support
  • Be patient - recovery takes time
  • Watch for suicide warning signs
  • Help them maintain routines and connections

Helpful Scriptures

  • Psalm 34:18 - God is close to the brokenhearted
  • 2 Corinthians 1:3-4 - God of all comfort
  • Isaiah 41:10 - Do not fear, I am with you

Anxiety Disorders

Types

  • Generalized Anxiety: Persistent worry about multiple areas
  • Panic Disorder: Recurrent panic attacks
  • Social Anxiety: Fear of social situations
  • Specific Phobias: Intense fear of specific objects/situations

Key Symptoms

  • Excessive worry difficult to control
  • Physical symptoms (racing heart, sweating, trembling)
  • Avoidance behaviors
  • Sleep difficulties
  • Panic attacks

Discipleship Considerations

  • Validate their experience - anxiety is real
  • Help them practice grounding techniques
  • Encourage gradual exposure to fears with support
  • Prayer can help but shouldn't replace treatment

Bipolar Disorder

Key Features

  • Manic Episodes: Elevated mood, decreased sleep, risky behavior
  • Depressive Episodes: Similar to major depression
  • Mixed Episodes: Features of both simultaneously

Warning Signs of Mania

  • Sleeping very little but not tired
  • Talking very fast, jumping between topics
  • Grandiose beliefs or plans
  • Increased religious preoccupation
  • Impulsive decisions

Critical: Medication Compliance

Bipolar disorder requires medication. Stopping medication often triggers episodes. Support medication compliance as part of stewardship of health.

Post-Traumatic Stress Disorder

Key Symptoms

  • Intrusive memories/flashbacks
  • Nightmares
  • Avoidance of trauma reminders
  • Negative changes in thoughts/mood
  • Hypervigilance
  • Emotional numbing

Discipleship Considerations

  • Create safety in your meetings
  • Be predictable and consistent
  • Avoid surprising them
  • Understand trauma affects faith
  • Professional trauma therapy is essential

Eating Disorders

Types

  • Anorexia Nervosa: Restriction, fear of weight gain
  • Bulimia Nervosa: Binge eating with purging
  • Binge Eating Disorder: Recurrent binge episodes

Warning Signs

  • Preoccupation with food, weight, body
  • Restricting food groups
  • Eating in secret
  • Bathroom visits after meals
  • Excessive exercise
  • Body image distortion

Critical

Eating disorders have the highest mortality rate of any mental illness. Medical monitoring is essential.

Borderline Personality Disorder

Key Features

  • Intense, unstable relationships
  • Fear of abandonment
  • Identity disturbance
  • Impulsivity
  • Suicidal behavior or self-harm
  • Emotional instability
  • Chronic emptiness

Discipleship Considerations

  • Maintain consistent boundaries
  • Avoid becoming their only support
  • Don't take splitting personally
  • Encourage DBT therapy
  • Be consistent and predictable

Discipleship Strategies

Supporting those with mental health challenges

Foundation Principles

  • Mental illness is not a spiritual failure - It's a medical condition like diabetes or heart disease
  • Faith and medicine work together - God can use doctors, therapy, and medication
  • Your role has limits - You're a discipler, not a therapist
  • Professional help is essential - Serious mental illness requires clinical treatment

Practical Guidelines

Meeting Structure

  • Keep consistent schedule and location
  • Start with check-in about their week
  • Be flexible with study material based on their capacity
  • End with specific prayer requests
  • Keep meetings to agreed timeframe

Communication

  • Use "I notice" statements rather than "You always"
  • Validate emotions without agreeing with distorted thoughts
  • Ask "What would be helpful?" rather than assuming
  • Text check-ins between meetings

Boundaries

  • You're not available 24/7
  • Don't become their therapist
  • Refer crisis calls to professionals
  • Maintain your own support system

Adapting Bible Study

When They're Struggling

  • Use shorter passages
  • Focus on God's love and presence
  • Avoid heavy conviction passages
  • Use creative methods (art, music, nature)
  • Let them just receive without sharing

Helpful Topics

  • God's unconditional love
  • Lament Psalms
  • God's presence in suffering
  • Biblical figures who struggled (Elijah, David)
  • Hope and future restoration

Topics to Approach Carefully

  • Spiritual warfare (can increase paranoia)
  • End times (can trigger anxiety)
  • Sin and judgment (when they're already self-condemning)
  • Joy and victory (when they're unable to feel positive emotions)

Supporting Medication Compliance

  • Frame medication as stewardship of their body/temple
  • Share that many strong Christians take medication
  • Don't suggest stopping medication
  • If they want to stop, encourage discussing with doctor
  • Notice and affirm when medication helps

Crisis Protocol

When and how to get emergency help

If someone is in immediate danger, contact emergency services immediately.

Don't wait. Don't try to handle it alone.

Suicide Risk Assessment

Warning Signs - Immediate Action Needed

  • Talking about wanting to die or kill themselves
  • Looking for ways to kill themselves
  • Talking about feeling hopeless or having no purpose
  • Talking about being a burden to others
  • Increasing use of alcohol or drugs
  • Withdrawing from activities and isolating
  • Giving away prized possessions
  • Saying goodbye to loved ones

What to Do

  1. Take it seriously - always
  2. Don't leave them alone
  3. Remove means (pills, weapons, etc.)
  4. Call crisis hotline or emergency services
  5. Go with them to emergency room if needed

When to Call Emergency Services

  • Suicide attempt or clear plan
  • Threat to harm others
  • Psychosis (seeing/hearing things)
  • Severe self-harm
  • Catatonia (unable to move/respond)
  • Severe mania with dangerous behavior

What to Tell Emergency Services

  • Person's name and location
  • Mental health diagnosis if known
  • Current medications
  • Specific concerning behaviors
  • Any weapons present

After a Crisis

  • Debrief with your own support system
  • Document what happened
  • Follow up with the person when appropriate
  • Connect them with ongoing professional help
  • Adjust your discipleship approach as needed
  • Consider your own capacity to continue

How to Use This App

Purpose

This app helps discipleship workers support young women with mental health challenges, especially in areas with limited professional resources.

Main Features

  • Find Professionals: Connect with Christian psychiatrists and therapists via teletherapy
  • Assessment Tools: Standard screening tools (PHQ-9, GAD-7) to understand severity
  • Diagnosis Info: Understanding common mental health conditions
  • Discipleship Guide: Practical strategies for supporting those with mental illness
  • Crisis Protocol: When and how to get emergency help
  • Resources: Books, training, and materials

Important Reminders

  • This app doesn't replace professional mental health care
  • Serious mental illness requires clinical treatment
  • Your role is discipleship, not therapy
  • When in doubt, refer to professionals

Getting Started

  1. Use the Quick Assessment on the home screen
  2. Based on results, explore relevant sections
  3. Connect with professionals for consultation
  4. Use the discipleship strategies while they get treatment

Install on Phone

iPhone: Share → Add to Home Screen

Android: Menu → Add to Home Screen
